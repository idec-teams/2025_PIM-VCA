Pour the solution into the gel tray and insert an appropriate comb. Allow the gel to solidify before use.

  • Observethe results.

 

  1. Gel Extraction of PCR Fragment
  • Excise the target band from the gel using a gel cutting instrument and transfer it to a 1.5 mL EP tube. Record the weight.
  • Add 3 volumes (approx. 600 μL) of Gel Dissolution Buffer PE to the tube. Incubate at 50°C to dissolve the gel, gently inverting the tube periodically until complete dissolution is achieved.
  • Transfer the dissolved gel solution to the Binding Column CA5 and incubate at room temperature for 5 minutes. Centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 60 seconds.
  • Transfer the flow-through from the collection tube back onto the same binding column. Incubate at room temperature for 5 minutes, then centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 60 seconds. Discard the flow-through.
  • Add 600 μL of Wash Buffer PW to the binding column. Incubate at room temperature for 5 minutes, then centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 60 seconds. Discard the waste liquid and place the binding column CA5 back into the collection tube.
  • Repeat the last
  • Centrifuge the binding column at 12,000 rpm for 2 minutes to remove residual wash buffer. Transfer the column to a 50°C oven for 5 minutes to complete drying.
  • Place the binding column into a new 1.5 mL EP tube. Add 30 μL of ddH₂O to the center of the binding membrane. Incubate at room temperature for 2 minutes, then centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 2 minutes to collect the DNA solution.
  • Reload the eluted DNA solution onto the binding membrane and let it stand at room temperature for 2 minutes. Centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 2 minutes. Measure the DNA concentration using a spectrophotometer.

Agarose Gel Electrophoresis

  • Preparation of 1% Agarose gel: Dissolve 0.6 g Agarose in 60 mL 1× TAE buffer. Heat until completely melted. After slight cooling, add 6 μL Gel Red nucleic acid dye. Pour the solution into a gel tray and insert an appropriate comb. Allow the gel to solidify prior to electrophoresis.

  • Visualize the results.

 

Gel Extraction of PCR Fragment

  • Excise the target DNA band using a gel cutting device. Transfer the gel slice to a 1.5 mL EP tube and record its weight.
  • Add 3 volumes (approximately 600 μL) of Gel Dissolution Buffer (PE) to the tube. Incubate at 50°C to dissolve the gel, gently inverting the tube intermittently to ensure complete dissolution.
  • Transfer the dissolved gel solution to the CA5 adsorption column. Incubate at room temperature for 5 minutes, then centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 60 seconds.
  • Reload the flow-through from the collection tube onto the same column. Incubate at room temperature for 5 minutes, then centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 60 seconds. Discard the flow-through.
  • Add 600 μL Wash Buffer (PW) to the column. Incubate at room temperature for 5 minutes, then centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 60 seconds. Discard the flow-through and reassemble the column with the collection tube.
  • Repeat the last
  • Centrifuge the empty column at 12,000 rpm for 2 minutes to remove residual ethanol. Transfer the column to a 50°C oven for 5 minutes to complete drying.
  • Place the column in a new 1.5 mL EP tube. Apply 30 μL of ddH₂O to the center of the adsorption membrane. Incubate at room temperature for 2 minutes, then centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 2 minutes to elute the DNA.
  • Reload the eluted DNA solution onto the same membrane. Incubate at room temperature for 2 minutes, then centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 2 minutes. Measure the DNA concentration using a spectrophotometer.

Protein induction for mp6-UDG4 mp6-tsPurple mp6-adA8e mp6-TadA8

  1. Inoculate 300 μL of overnight seed culture into 50 mL of medium containing antibiotic. After 3 hours, measure the OD600, which should be between 0.6 and 0.8.
  2. Add IPTG to a final concentration of 0.5 mM.
  3. Induce at 37°C for 16 hours. Centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 1 minute, remove the supernatant, and store the pellet at -20°C.

 

Transformation: Add 1 μL of each plasmid to BL21 competent cells.

  1. Prepare a mixture of 400 μL Tris-HCl (pH=8.0) and 400 μL 2x SDS-PAGE loading buffer.
  2. Resuspend pelleted cells in 20 μL of the above mixture and heat in a boiling water bath for 5 minutes.
  3. Centrifuge at 15,000 rpm for 30 minutes and collect the supernatant. Load 20 μL onto an SDS-PAGE gel. Run at 90 V for 40 minutes, then at 120 V for 20 minutes.
  4. Stain with Coomassie Brilliant Blue for 15 minutes, followed by overnight destaining.
  5. Resuspend cell pellet in 20 mL PBS. Place in an ice-water bath and sonicate using a 60% duty cycle: 10 seconds on, 10 seconds off, for a total of 10 minutes.Centrifuge at 13,000 × g for 30 minutes at 4°C. Store the supernatant (soluble fraction) at -20°C.

Induction comparison for mp6-tsPurple:

Inoculate a single colony into 100 mL of medium containing antibiotic. Prepare two flasks: one serving as a control (no inducer added) and one supplemented with 1% L-arabinose. Induce at 37°C for 16 hours. Centrifuge at 8,000 rpm for 3 minutes, remove the supernatant, and store the pellet at -20°C.

 

Co-transformation experiment:

  • Retrieve DH5α competent cells from the -80°C freezer. Thaw the cells on ice for 5 minutes. Add 5 μL of each plasmid to the cells and incubate on ice for 30 minutes.
  • Subject the cells to a heat shock at 42°C for 90 seconds, then immediately return them to ice for 2 minutes.
  • Under a sterile laminar flow hood, add 900 μL of LB liquid medium without antibiotic. Incubate the culture at 37°C with shaking at 200 rpm for 1 hour.
  • Under the sterile hood, centrifuge the cells to pellet them (8,000 rpm for 5 minutes). Resuspend the pellet by pipetting. Plate 50 μL of the resuspended culture onto an LB agar plate containing the appropriate antibiotic(s). Incubate the plate upside down at 37°C for 14-16 hours.

 

For overnight culture analysis: Take the overnight seed culture, perform a 1000-fold dilution, and spread the dilution on chloramphenicol-containing plates. Incubate overnight and observe the results.
